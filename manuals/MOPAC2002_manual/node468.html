Eigenvectors

Conventional M.O.s are one-electron solutions to Schrödinger's equation, and can be studied by photoelectron and other methods. Such methods work on a timescale of about 10-17s, roughly the time it takes light to cross a molecule. Normal chemical methods (study of reactions, etc.) yield little information about conventional M.O.s. Some information is obtained by inference, such as aromatic substitution directing considerations.
