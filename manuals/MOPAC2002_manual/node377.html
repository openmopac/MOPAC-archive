Another way of looking at this is to recognize that, in H2, electron density between the atoms is greater when a bond exists than when it does not exist. Given this, it follows that, in order to ensure that the total number of electrons in the system remains constant when a bond forms, at some other point in space the electron density must be less when a bond exists than when it does not exist. In other words, if density builds up in some region of space, then it must become less somewhere else in order that the total number of electrons would remain constant.
