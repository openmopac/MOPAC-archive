Construction of Non-Cyclic p Bonds

A potential complication exists when the p bonds are constructed, in that it is possible to start constructing p bonds in such a way that a di-radical is formed. For example, in butadiene, if a p bond is constructed between carbon atoms 2 and 3, then radical sites would be formed at atoms 1 and 4. To avoid this, the first set of p bonds constructed is that set in which the way the p bond is drawn is unambiguous. The rule for assigning p bonds is simple: a p bond is constructed if an atom can make a p bond with exactly one other atom.

In benzaldehyde, this situation exists only for the carbon and the oxygen of the -CHO group. The p bonds in the ring cannot be assigned using this rule, because every atom in the ring can potentially make a p bond with two other atoms. In more complicated systems, repeated application of the rule automatically results in the correct assignment of p bonds in all non-cycle conjugated systems.

As with the s bonds, when a p bond is made, two electrons and two atomic orbitals are used up, and an occupied and an unoccupied LMO are formed. After the non-cyclic p bonds are formed, the number of available orbitals and electrons is as shown in the Table.
