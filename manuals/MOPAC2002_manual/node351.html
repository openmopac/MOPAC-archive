Conversion to Cartesian Coordinates

Normally, atom 1 is at the origin of Cartesian coordinate space. However, if atom 1 has non-zero coordinates, then it will be at the position defined by its coordinates. Be careful, however, if atom 1 is a dummy atom. Atom 2 is defined as being displaced in the X-direction from atom 1 by a distance equal to its bond length. Atom 3 is in the X-Y plane of atoms 1 and 2 unless the angle 3-2-1 is exactly 0 or 180 degrees. Atom 4, 5, 6, etc. can lie anywhere in 3-D space.
