Difference Maps

A difference map shows where electron density builds up when bonds are formed. It will NOT show ionic character. To form a BONDS map the electron density arising from the atoms is spherically averaged and then subtracted from the density matrix. For example, suppose the atomic orbital populations on an atom arising from the un-renormalized eigenvectors are 1.0, 1.1, 1.2, and 1.3 for s, px, py, and pz, respectively. Then the spherically averaged populations are 1.0, 1.2, 1.2, and 1.2 for these atomic orbitals.
