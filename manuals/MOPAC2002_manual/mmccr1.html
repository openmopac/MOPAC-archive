MMCCR1=n.nn

NDDO semiempirical methods predict some organic ring systems, such as cyclopentane, to be planar, although they are known to be puckered.  To correct for this, a molecular mechanics correction can be applied.  The default form of this correction is:

DHf' = DHf + 4.0e-(angle/20.0)**2

where "angle" is the dihedral or torsion angle between four consecutive sp3 carbon atoms in a five membered ring.  The multiplier, 4.0 here, can be replaced by n.nn by use of MMCCR1=n.nn.  See also MMCCR2 and MMCCROK.  In cyclopentane, there are five sets of atoms like this, so five corrections would be made.
