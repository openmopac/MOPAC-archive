Partial Geometry Optimization

When localized molecular orbitals are used, the M.O.s associated with each atom can readily be identified. This means that when a partial geometry optimization is run, only the LMOs involved in the moving atoms need be used. A consequence of this is that such calculations run much faster. For large systems, in which only a few atoms move, the largest increase in speed over conventional methods was a factor of 13,000 times.
