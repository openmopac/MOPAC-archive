Atomic bond index

A measure of the valency of an atom.


Typical valencies are: 1.0 for hydrogen, 2.0-2.4 for oxygen to 3.8-4.0 for carbon. The maximum valency of an atom is equal to the number of atomic orbitals, e.g. 1 or 4 (for a sp3 system), or 9 (in atoms with d orbitals). This maximum is only achieved when the orbital population is 1.00, and all off-diagonal terms on the atom are zero.
