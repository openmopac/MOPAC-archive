Density Around Atoms

All Slater atomic orbitals have a node at the nucleus, with the important exception of hydrogen. One result of this is that hydrogen is the only element whose electron density has a maximum at the nucleus. All other atoms have an almost zero electron density at their nucleus. This is usually disconcerting at first sight, but is a consequence of the behavior of Slater-type orbitals. For planar systems, it is a good idea to plot the density in the plane a fraction of an Ångstrom above the plane of the molecule.
