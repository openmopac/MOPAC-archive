Construction of Cyclic Bonds

 At this point, the only remaining bonds are those in cyclic p systems. The sequence in which these are assigned is similar to that for the non-cyclic p bonds. One of the remaining p bonds is assigned arbitrarily, then the rule for assigning non-cyclic p bonds is used until no more p bonds can be made. This two-step sequence is repeated until all cyclic p systems have been accounted for.

In the case of benzaldehyde, this completes the construction of the occupied and virtual starting LMOs. Two more structures are possible in enzymes, however. These will be considered next.
