Vibrations (Phonon Spectrum)

As with molecules, the vibrational spectrum of polymers can be calculated. However, unlike molecular normal modes, which have quantized vibrational frequencies, polymers give rise to bands of frequencies, defined by the associated wave-vector k in the Brillouin zone. At k=0, four of these bands have zero frequency, corresponding to the four trivial vibrations of a polymer.
