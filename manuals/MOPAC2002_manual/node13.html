Technical, Compatibility,  and Other Changes

As a result of the steady accumulation of donations, MOPAC had become quite difficult to modify. This had tended to discourage users from trying to add new features. To correct this, a major re-write of the program has been done. The new program is written in FORTRAN 90.  In this, the COMMON blocks have been replaced by modules.

Two entities have been modified.  In MOPAC 2000, there were the symbols Na and K.  In AM1 and in PM3, these symbols represented "sparkles" - 100% ionized alkali metal ions.  In MOPAC 2002, these sparkles have been replaced by atoms with full s-p basis sets.  In addition to being more realistic, the new entities are now of quantitative use.

The band-structure keyword "K=n.nn" has been deleted.  Previously, it was provided to generate band-structures of polymers. With the extension of the polymer options to layer and solid systems, it became more appropriate to do all band structure work in the utility BZ.
