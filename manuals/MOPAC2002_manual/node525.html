Construction of Sigma Framework

Because it is the simplest set of bonds to identify, the s framework is constructed first. s bonds are defined as existing between every pair of atoms which are chemically bonded together. In our example, there are 14 s bonds. Each di-atomic s bond is constructed by identifying which pair of unused hybrid orbitals on the atoms involved has the largest interaction energy. When one of the atoms is a hydrogen, only the hybrid orbitals on the other atom need to be examined at this point.

Once the appropriate orbitals have been identified, bonding and antibonding diatomic LMOs are constructed. This is accomplished by diagonalizing the associated two by two secular matrix. The elements of this matrix are shown in the Figure.

Figure: Secular Equation used in Constructing Starting LMOs
 

<Hybrid1|H|Hybrid1> - E11 <Hybrid1|H|Hybrid2>
<Hybrid2|H|Hybrid1> <Hybrid2|H|Hybrid2> - E22

For every s bond formed, two electrons are used, one from each of the atoms involved in forming the bond, and two atomic orbitals are used - again, one from each atom. After all 14 s bonds are formed, the number of still unused atomic orbitals and electrons is much reduced. The remaining unused atomic orbitals and electrons are shown in the Table. The oxygen forms only one s bond, so there are still three unused hybrid orbitals and five valence electrons left.
